History

Calligraphy likely originated in ancient China, where characters were initially carved on materials like animal bones and tortoise shells. Chinese Ink Brush: The practice evolved to using Chinese ink brushes and writing on paper. The term "calligraphy" finds its roots in the Greek language, where it signifies "to write with beauty." Chinese calligraphy subsequently influenced neighboring regions like Japan and Korea, where people developed their unique calligraphic styles, much like contemporary calligraphers do today.

Categories of calligraphy

Traditional Calligrphy
Traditional Calligraphy
Traditional calligraphy is a disciplined art form of hand lettering that follows specific rules and techniques for creating precise, legible, and beautiful letterforms.Classical calligraphy differs from typography and non-classical hand-lettering, though a calligrapher may create all of these; characters are historically disciplined yet fluid and spontaneous, improvised at the moment of writing.
Contemporary Calligraphy
Contemporary Calligraphy
It is the design and execution of lettering with a pen, ink brush, or other writing instrument. Contemporary calligraphic practice can be defined as "the art of giving form to signs in an expressive, harmonious, and skillful manner.In the contemporary era, calligraphy has seen a resurgence, blending traditional techniques with modern tools and mediums.
Hand-Lettering & Designs
Hand-Lettering & Designs
hand lettering is the art of hand drawing letters, and calligraphy is the art of writing. Lettering artists don't need a special tool to create their work, while calligraphers use a special calligraphy pen.Hand lettering focuses on drawing and illustrating the letters.Unlike calligraphy, where we write the letters, with hand lettering, we are drawing the individual letterforms.
Modren Calligraphy
Modren Calligraphy
Its literal meaning is beautiful writing. Calligraphy is a single pass with a pen to write letters. It uses the same lettering style throughout, so you will need to develop muscle memory to create calligraphy. Even with a modern calligraphy style, you still need to pay some attention to structure.

Forms & Styles

Broad Edge

Broad edge calligraphy can be executed perfectly if you use flat nib pens. Broad edge pens give your calligraphy and hand lettering projects a nice contrast, with both thin and very thick lines.

Uncial

Uncial is a majuscule script commonly used from the 4th to 8th centuries AD by Latin and Greek scribes. Uncial letters were used to write Greek and Latin, as well as Gothic, and are the current style for Coptic and Nobiin.

Seal

Seal script or sigillary script is a style of writing Chinese characters that was common throughout the latter half of the 1st millennium BC. It evolved organically out of bronze script during the Zhou dynasty.

Riqa‘

“Riqa‘ ” is derived from the Arabic noun, ruq’a, meaning “a patch or piece of cloth.” The script was named thus because it was often written on small pieces of paper to petition royalty.
